Workout Wiki

Our recommendations come in 4 different flavors:
HIIT WORKOUT

HIIT - “High intensity, interval training” involves intense bursts of exercise followed by periods of rest, aimed at repeatedly increasing then decreasing your heart rate over the course of a workout. Circuit training is a common example.
CARDIO

Cardio - specifically “steady state cardio” is less intense than HIIT and is designed to elevate your heart rate to a lesser extent but for a more prolonged period of time. This type of workout increases your cardiovascular endurance. Jogging or jumping rope is a good example.
STRENGTH

Strength workouts - basically moving your limbs against resistance. The classic example is lifting weights in the gym but don't worry - you can get just as good a workout using household objects or your own body weight.

FLEXIBILITY

Now that we’re all stuck inside, maintaining a good range of motion in your joints and muscles is more important than ever. If you’re feeling spicy and want to add an extra workout to your routine, flexibility is the way to go.
